The Work

Choose one sentence from your worksheet — the one that still feels most painful. Work it through Byron Katie's four questions, then turn it around.

Work with the sentence exactly as you wrote it. There are no right or wrong answers.
The Four Questions
Q1
Is it true?
Answer yes or no.
Q2
Can you absolutely know it's true?
Can you know with 100% certainty?
Q3
How do you react when you believe that thought?
How do you treat yourself and others? What do you do?
Q4
Who would you be without that thought?
Imagine the same situation — but without the ability to think this thought.
Turn it Around

Turnarounds

Turn the painful sentence around in three ways. For each, find three genuine examples where the turnaround might be as true as your original thought.

The Opposite
Reverse the statement. e.g. "My boss does not ignore my efforts." Find three examples.
Turn it to the Self
Replace "my boss" with "I." e.g. "I ignore my own efforts." Three examples.
Turn it to the Other
e.g. "My boss ignores his own efforts." Three examples.
